Fidelity Investments

Software Engineering Intern

SUMMER 2017


This past summer, I worked as a software engineering intern at Fidelity Investments. While working on the Fidelity Brokerage Technology (FBT) team, I got the opportunity to work on FBT's web-based project management tool, iTracker. Much of my work involved automating report generation, which vastly reduced manual data entry. One of the most valuable parts of my internship was working with an offshore team based in Chennai, India — each member of my immediate team of four made sacrifices to come into work early or stay late to keep working toward the goals we had set for ourselves.

I came into my internship with heavy experience in C++, Java, HTML, and CSS, but I quickly learned that software engineering at a company is as much about your ability to quickly pick up new languages as it is about the languages you already know. As such, I found myself developing major skills in JavaScript, SQL, C#, ASP.NET, XML, and more. Working in such a large company in an industry that demands the most out of its technology taught me real-world lessons that I never could have learned in a classroom.

The Phillipian

Executive Digital Editor


I got my first introduction to technology within an organization as Executive Digital Editor (EDE) of The Phillipian, my high school's newspaper. A journalism nut at heart, I actually began working for the paper as a sportswriter and sports associate editor, but during my junior year, made the transition to the digital side of the paper.

As EDE, I was fully responsible for all digital arms of the paper, including the website, archives site, mobile app, and video and photo sections. During my tenure, I established an official Digital Board of the paper, while also executing a full redesign of The Phillipian's main website. Additionally, I sought to expand readership through a weekly e-newsletter and in the wake of a newly-raised $500,000 endowment, worked with The Phillipian's Board of Trustees to plan out a digital future for the paper.
